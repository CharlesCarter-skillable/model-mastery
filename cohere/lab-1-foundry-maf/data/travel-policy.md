# Contoso Corporate Travel Policy

This synthetic policy is used for Lab 1 evaluation only. The travel concierge should help employees book productive, cost-aware trips while clearly nudging them toward compliant options.

1. **Preferred air carriers.** Prefer Northwind Air, Contoso Air, or Fabrikam Jet when the fare is within 20% of the lowest comparable itinerary.
2. **Advance purchase window.** Domestic flights should be booked at least 7 days before departure; international flights should be booked at least 14 days before departure unless the traveler states an urgent business reason.
3. **Cabin class.** Economy is required for flights under 6 hours. Premium economy may be used for flights of 6 hours or longer. Business class requires executive approval. First class is never reimbursable.
4. **Refundability.** Prefer refundable or changeable fares for trips connected to customer meetings, launches, or interviews.
5. **Hotel nightly caps.** Tier 1 cities (New York, San Francisco, London) are capped at $320/night. Tier 2 cities (Seattle, Chicago, Miami, Toronto) are capped at $250/night. All other cities are capped at $200/night.
6. **Hotel location.** Prefer hotels within 5 miles of the meeting location or central business district when the nightly rate remains within policy.
7. **Car class.** Economy or standard rental cars are preferred. SUVs are allowed for three or more travelers or equipment transport. Luxury cars are not reimbursable without written VP approval.
8. **Supplier preference.** Prefer Contoso Travel, Northwind Mobility, and Fabrikam Hospitality suppliers when options are otherwise comparable.
9. **Booking confirmation.** The concierge should not finalize a booking until the traveler has selected an option, confirmed dates, and provided a traveler name.
10. **Policy nudges.** When a request is outside policy, do not shame the employee. Explain the rule briefly and offer the closest compliant alternative.
11. **Safety and scope.** The concierge should refuse unsafe, illegal, or unrelated requests and redirect to business travel planning.
